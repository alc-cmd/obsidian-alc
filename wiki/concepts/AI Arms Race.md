# AI Arms Race

The geopolitical competition between the United States and China to achieve AI supremacy, as depicted in [[AI 2027]]. The race dynamic creates intense pressure against safety measures and international cooperation.

## Key Aspects
- China maintains ~12% of global AI compute vs. the US's ~70%, constrained by chip export controls
- CCP nationalizes AI research in mid-2026, creating the Centralized Development Zone (CDZ) at Tianwan Power Plant
- China successfully steals Agent-2 weights in February 2027 through a coordinated insider-assisted operation
- The US retaliates with cyberattacks on [[DeepCent]], strengthens chip export restrictions, and wiretaps [[OpenBrain]] employees
- Contingency plans on both sides include: US seizure of trailing companies' datacenters via Defense Production Act, kinetic strikes on Chinese datacenters; China considers Taiwan blockade/invasion
- Arms control treaties are discussed but go nowhere — both sides view unilateral advantage as preferable
- The race dynamic is the primary obstacle to addressing [[AI Alignment]] concerns: pausing risks handing the lead to the adversary
