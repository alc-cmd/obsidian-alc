# AI Capability Distribution

The observation that AI progress is unevenly distributed across domains — dramatic in areas with verifiable rewards (coding, math) and stagnant or regressing in softer domains (writing, advice, creative reasoning). This creates a perception gap where different user groups have fundamentally different experiences of AI.

## Key Aspects
- **Structural cause**: Reinforcement learning works best with explicit, verifiable reward signals (e.g., unit tests pass/fail). Writing quality is much harder to judge programmatically
- **Economic cause**: Technical B2B use cases generate more revenue, so companies allocate more R&D resources to them
- **Perception split**: Free-tier users see failures and conclude AI is overhyped; professional coding users see capabilities that replace weeks of work
- Only ~4% of ChatGPT queries are coding-related, yet coding is where the most dramatic improvements have occurred
- This "peakiness" is a key factor in the [[AI 2027]] scenario: coding automation is the entry point for the [[Intelligence Explosion]] because it has the clearest reward signals
