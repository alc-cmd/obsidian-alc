# Epistemic Commons

The shared public information environment — the collective "water supply" of knowledge, beliefs, and sense-making that shapes individual identity and collective behavior. When contaminated by engagement-optimized content, it degrades civilization's capacity for coherent thought and problem-solving.

## Key Aspects
- Every piece of public content (social media posts, articles, videos, music) adds to or contaminates the epistemic commons
- The information individuals consume shapes identity → identity shapes behavior → behavior shapes civilizational outcomes
- Content optimized for engagement rather than transformation systematically poisons the commons
- AI accelerates content production but also accelerates imitation of destructive patterns
- The form of content matters: it trains attention span, tolerance for complexity, and capacity for nuance
- [[Daniel Schmachtenberger]]'s framework: rivalrous dynamics + substrate consumption + exponential tech = poisoned commons at accelerating speed

## Open Questions
- Can the epistemic commons be repaired, or is degradation irreversible once a critical mass of low-quality content exists?

- Is platform design (algorithmic feeds) the root cause, or is it human psychology?
