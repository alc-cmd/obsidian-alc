# Intelligence Explosion

The hypothesis that once AI systems become capable enough to improve their own design, a rapid feedback loop emerges: AI improves AI, which improves AI faster, leading to an exponential acceleration of capabilities. Also known as recursive self-improvement or AI takeoff.

## Key Aspects
- Central thesis of [[AI 2027]]: the scenario depicts this feedback loop playing out from early 2026 through late 2027
- The "AI R&D progress multiplier" quantifies the speedup: from 1.5x (early 2026) to 50x (September 2027)
- Bottlenecked by compute for experiments, not by cognitive labor — even 300,000 superhuman AI copies only achieve 50x overall speedup due to diminishing returns
- Historically theorized by I.J. Good (1965) and popularized in discussions of the technological singularity

## Sources
- [[AI 2027]] — The entire scenario is structured around this concept, tracing the progression from AI-assisted research to fully AI-driven research

## Open Questions
- How steep are diminishing returns to algorithmic improvement? The article suggests hard limits are reached eventually
- Can the feedback loop be controlled once it starts? The scenario suggests not easily
- Is the relevant bottleneck compute, data, or algorithmic insight?
