# Meaning Economy

The emerging economic landscape where meaning — the experience of ordered consciousness — becomes the scarcest and most valuable commodity. As AI and algorithms flood the information environment with fast content, genuine sense-making becomes rare and therefore premium.

## Key Aspects
- **Meaning as ordered consciousness**: Drawing on Csikszentmihalyi's flow theory, meaning emerges when attention is invested in a complex, challenging activity with clear feedback (psychic negentropy)
- **Fast content creates entropy**: Pre-digested, algorithmic content skips the ordering process and leaves consciousness disordered — informed but empty
- **Slow content creates negentropy**: Essays and genuine thinking require both writer and reader to engage in ordering consciousness
- **The economic opportunity**: As fast content becomes commoditized (especially by AI), slow content that produces genuine meaning commands premium value
- **Historical arc**: From praising gods → productivity worship → "more is God" → meaning becomes the next scarce good
