# Stack Declaration Post

A short-form post in which the author declares how they use multiple tools, each assigned a role or identity metaphor. Reads as a personal manifesto compressed into three or four parallel sentences.

## Form

> *"ChatGPT is my doctor. Claude is my coworker. Cursor is my dev. I love all of them."* — [[AlexAiSurfer]], 2026-04-17

Structure: `<Tool A> is my <Role A>. <Tool B> is my <Role B>. <Tool C> is my <Role C>. <Tagline>.`

## Why it works

1. **Identity projection.** The post is really about the author's operating model, not the tools. Readers get a 10-second snapshot of how a serious user composes their AI stack.
2. **Parallel structure.** The repeated "X is my Y" cadence is memorable and rhythmically scannable in-feed.
3. **Taste signal.** Picking *which* tool gets *which* role is the opinion — the post is a compressed taste demonstration.
4. **Triggers reply debate.** Others want to share their own mapping. High reply rate on small impression base.

## Evidence

From [[X Analytics Q1-Q2 2026]]:
- "ChatGPT is my doctor. Claude is my coworker. Cursor is my dev." — 172 impressions, 28 engagements → **16.3% ER**, top 3 by ER among originals in the period
- Drove 4 profile visits — identity posts outperform tool-explainers for profile conversion on a per-impression basis

## Application

- Ship one per month; overuse dilutes the signal.
- Keep to three items. Four is too many to hold in one glance.
- The metaphors should be *different domains* (doctor, coworker, dev) — don't pick three job titles from the same field.
- End with a one-beat tagline (*"I love all of them"*, *"all essential"*) — signals affection, not comparison.

## Anti-patterns

- "X > Y > Z" ranking posts — invite hostile replies and feel like bait.
- Five-plus items — reads as a roster, not a declaration.
- Obscure tools nobody knows — the metaphor needs shared ground to land.
