# I'm Begging You to Write More Essays

[[Dan Koe]] argues that essays are one of the last forms of content that develop real thinking capacity, and that the internet's optimization for engagement is destroying civilization's ability to make sense of reality. He draws heavily on [[Daniel Schmachtenberger]]'s metacrisis framework and Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi's concept of ordered consciousness.

## Key Takeaways
- The epistemic commons (public information environment) is being poisoned by content optimized for engagement over transformation
- Three civilizational threats converge: rivalrous dynamics, substrate consumption (attention/cognition), and exponential technology
- Essays are distinct from articles: articles package existing knowledge, essays are acts of thinking that change the author's beliefs
- AI cannot write a true essay — it lacks a situated point of view, direct experience, and the capacity for surprise and discovery
- The "meaning economy" is emerging: meaning (ordered consciousness) is the scarcest commodity, and those who create it will thrive
- Fast content (pre-digested, algorithmic) creates psychic entropy; slow content (essays, genuine thinking) creates psychic negentropy

## Summary

Koe builds his argument in five layers. First, the epistemic commons — our shared information environment — is being contaminated by content that looks like thinking but structurally cannot produce it. Second, drawing on Schmachtenberger's metacrisis framework, three forces (rivalrous dynamics, substrate consumption, exponential tech) are converging toward either civilizational collapse or dystopian control, with a third attractor of shared sense-making as the positive alternative.

Third, essays represent "slow content" — the opposite of engagement-optimized fast content. The key distinction: articles deliver conclusions, essays discover them. Only humans can write essays because they require a situated perspective shaped by lived experience. Fourth, the meaning economy is emerging because meaning itself is scarce. Meaning arises from ordering consciousness (psychic negentropy), and essays create this for both writer and reader. Fifth, practical advice: write to discover, resist templates, challenge your own beliefs, and build a body of work rather than a content calendar.

## Notable Claims
- AI "destroys the magic of surprise and discovery" — anticipating novelty eliminates its creative value
- The form of content you consume trains your attention span, tolerance for complexity, and capacity for nuance
- Content that "looks like it should shift your thinking but structurally cannot" is the core problem
- Essays are "the most scalable and durable form" of meaningful content — conversations die in memory, essays persist for decades
- "99% of people don't even know what [civilizational] problems entail, because they're happy drooling over the cat video on their phone"
