# X Analytics Export — Q1-Q2 2026

Official X (Twitter) Analytics export covering [[AlexAiSurfer]]'s account activity from **2026-01-24 to 2026-04-23** (90 days). CSV contains 52 posts with full engagement columns: impressions, engagements, likes, reposts, replies, bookmarks, shares, new follows, profile visits, detail expands, URL clicks, hashtag clicks, permalink clicks.

Basis for [[AlexAiSurfer X Engagement]] analysis.

## Scope

- **Period:** 2026-01-24 → 2026-04-23 (90 days)
- **Posts covered:** 52 total (21 originals + 31 @replies)
- **Raw totals:** 5,910 impressions, 617 engagements, 9 new follows
- **Export source:** analytics.x.com → Content → Export data

## Key Takeaways
- 83.7% of impressions come from original posts (40% of posting volume). @replies are net-negative for reach.
- W15 (Apr 6–12) was the breakout week: 22 posts → 3,380 impressions → 6 new follows. Volume drives reach.
- Top post (1,552 impressions, Apr 10) was media-only — visual pulls 10× the reach of text.
- Tool-discovery posts with number receipts ("155k stars", "1 in 7") consistently hit 12–18% engagement rate.
- Overall follower conversion is 0.15% (9 follows / 5,910 impressions) — content resonates with existing audience but isn't breaking out.

## Summary

The CSV shows a clear performance bimodal: original posts with concrete-number hooks carry the quarter's reach while a high volume of @replies consumes posting bandwidth without meaningful impression or follower return. Account is verified, backed by [[Minara]], and targets AI/Web3/trading-workflow content. Top-performing themes: [[Minara]] product posts with data (TradingView strategy crawl), AI tool discovery (GBrain, Recordly, ChatGPT prompts repo), personal AI stack declarations, and timely platform-reaction posts (Codex, Anthropic agentic stack). Philosophical/evergreen posts ("Read slow") get moderate reach but low engagement rate. Reply-heavy days are dead weight.

## Notable Claims (from the data)
- 9 new follows in 90 days at ~499 follower base implies <1 new follower per week — growth lever is under-pulled.
- Single media-only post outperformed next 3 text posts combined.
- Engagement rate of 10.44% overall is strong for the account size, but inflated by small denominators on reply posts.
